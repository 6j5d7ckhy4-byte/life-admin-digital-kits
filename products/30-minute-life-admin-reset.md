# The 30-Minute Life Admin Reset

A simple reset for people who feel behind on bills, paperwork, appointments, subscriptions, taxes, household tasks, and family follow-ups.

**Purpose:** personal organization only. This is not legal, tax, medical, or financial advice.

## Before you start: privacy and safety

Do not put full Social Security numbers, passwords, private keys, seed phrases, full account numbers, or sensitive medical/legal details into casual notes. Keep sensitive documents in a secure place.

## What you need

- A timer for 30 minutes
- Paper, notes app, or spreadsheet
- One folder or digital folder called `Life Admin`
- Optional: three subfolders called `Bills`, `Taxes`, and `Important Documents`

## The 30-minute reset

### Minutes 0–5: Brain dump

Write everything that is taking up mental space:

- Bills to pay or check
- Documents to find
- Appointments to schedule
- Emails to send
- Subscriptions to cancel or review
- Household repairs
- Family tasks
- Tax documents or questions
- Money questions to ask a professional

Do not organize yet. Just capture.

### Minutes 5–10: Sort into buckets

Move each item into one of these buckets:

1. Bills and subscriptions
2. Taxes and documents
3. Health and appointments
4. Household and repairs
5. Family and relationships
6. Money and planning questions
7. Emails and follow-ups
8. Someday / not this week

### Minutes 10–15: Find quick wins

Pick anything that takes under 5 minutes:

- Cancel one unused subscription
- Pay or schedule one bill
- Put one appointment on the calendar
- Send one follow-up email
- Create one folder for documents
- Rename one messy file

### Minutes 15–20: Make a document checklist

Create a short list of documents to gather:

- ID documents
- Insurance info
- Recent bills
- Bank or credit-card statements
- Tax forms
- Receipts for major expenses
- Medical appointment notes
- Home or car records
- Family contact info

### Minutes 20–25: Choose this week’s top 3

Pick only three next actions:

1. ______________________________
2. ______________________________
3. ______________________________

A good next action starts with a verb: call, email, find, pay, schedule, scan, compare, ask, review.

### Minutes 25–30: Set your next reset

Choose when you will do this again:

- Weekly: best for busy seasons
- Twice monthly: best for normal life admin
- Monthly: best once things are stable

Next reset date: ______________________________

## Mini checklists

### Bill and subscription check

- [ ] Which bills are due in the next 14 days?
- [ ] Any annual renewals coming up?
- [ ] Any subscriptions I forgot about?
- [ ] Any bill higher than usual?
- [ ] Any payment method expiring soon?

### Tax organization check

- [ ] Create a tax folder for the year
- [ ] Save income documents
- [ ] Save charitable giving records
- [ ] Save major medical, home, education, or business-related receipts if relevant
- [ ] Make a list of questions for a tax professional

### Health appointment prep

This is for organization only, not medical advice.

- [ ] Appointment date and provider
- [ ] Main questions
- [ ] Current medications or supplements, if relevant
- [ ] Symptoms or concerns to discuss
- [ ] Documents or test results to bring
- [ ] Follow-up instructions after the visit

### Family follow-up check

- [ ] Who do I need to call, text, or email?
- [ ] Any birthdays, anniversaries, or events coming up?
- [ ] Any family documents, photos, or stories I want to preserve?
- [ ] Any support someone needs from me this week?
